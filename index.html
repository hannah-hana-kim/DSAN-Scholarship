Vegetation Status at Natural Bridge National Park

[Figure 1.1] Greenery Area in 1980
[Figure 1.2] Greenery Area in 2018
[Figure 1.3] Greenery Area in 2024

Figure 1.1 and Figure 1.2 are the maps that shows the greenery area at Natural Bridge National Part in 1980 and 2018 respectively. The greenery area in the northeast part of the park had been increased, whereas that of the middle, southern part had been decreased, although it is a slight difference.
Based on the data from 1980 to 2018, the organization predicted the same data from 2021 to 2024. Figure 1.3 shows the predicted greenery area in 2024. Even though it is a slight change, there are some interesting foundings from this chart.
The greenery area has been increased in the northern park of the park and the bare area has been recovered in the west area.

Plant Litter Status at Natural Bridge National Park

[Figure 2]

After analyzing the greenery area, I also wanted to deep dive into plant litter variable. This scatter plot shows the relationship between the percentage area of the plant litter and the annual temperature.
According to the article: Litter decomposition, effects of temperature driven by soil moisture and vegetation type, the plant litter has both direct and indirect relationship with temperature. The article tells us that the temperature will likely to decrease when there are more plant litter. This line chart proves this information. This chart included both historical data and predicted data, and it also predicted that the temperature will decrease in 2024.
